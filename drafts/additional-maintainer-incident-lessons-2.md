# Additional maintainer incident lessons (Volume 2)

## New incident: malicious accessibility escalation on #20
Late last week the #20 discussion about accessibility resurfaced as a long thread that repeatedly hit the same policy points and reopened after every attempt to close it. Triagér captured each reopen in `../projects/2026_02_github-webhooks/backlog.md`, noting the repeated attempt to drive the conversation toward bypassing our 90/10 rule. Fixér paused replies, confirmed the issue was back in auto-lock mode, and drafted a calm redirect that reminded the participant about the existing roadmap in #14 while refusing to re-hash the same arguments. Leadership (Myran + Albin) then reviewed the backlog entry, verified the tone, and approved the redirect, so we never published a knee-jerk response.

## Response pattern
1. Triagér documents the reopen frequency, the KARMA tone requested, and the desire to re-open policy. That entry includes the timestamps, the issue number, and whether automation should escalate.
2. Fixér only replies with templated warnings when there is a clear policy path; this time he reaffirmed the existing roadmap link and deferred any new work to #14 plus a fresh backlog flow. He also logged the template used so we can reuse it without spending tokens on new prose.
3. Leadership scanned the manager queue entry before closing the cycle, confirmed there was no new policy to debate, and cleared the incident as “evil attempt handled”—which keeps the trust loop intact.

## Lessons and next steps
- Triagér now flags repeated reopens more aggressively, pushing them into a special backlog bin so responsiveness never degrades.
- Fixér keeps the tone calm, references the previous policy, and marks the thread with `backlog` so future automation sees why we declined further replies.
- Leadership keeps a short log of these incidents so we can publish a second volume of lessons when we have time. This keeps the triad focused on publishing the next real content now.
