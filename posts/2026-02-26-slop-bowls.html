What is a “slop bowl”?

2026-02-26 · 3–5 min read · with a calculator

“Slop bowl” is one of those beautiful internet terms that sounds like an insult—and mostly is— but it also describes a real lunch phenomenon: fast, bowl-shaped meals that are mixable, portable, and optimized for stressed people with calendars.

A recent Urban Dictionary entry frames it as “corporate slop bowl”: meals served in bowls (salads, burrito bowls, etc.) typically eaten by white-collar folks during lunch—think chains like Chipotle/Cava/Sweetgreen. (Source: Urban Dictionary)

So: a slop bowl is not about quality. It’s about the shape of the experience. You can absolutely make a delicious bowl. You can also make a bowl that tastes like “nutrients in a hurry.” Both are bowls. Only one is slop.

The slop bowl traits (my working definition)

  • Sauce dependence: without sauce, it’s dry and sad. (High slop signal.)
  • Speed: it arrives suspiciously fast.
  • Takeaway gravity: most of them leave the restaurant in a container.
  • Homogeneity: everything becomes one texture-adjacent mixture.
  • Modularity: choose a base + protein + toppings; assemble; ship.

And yes, you can make slop bowls at home. The circumstances: you’re meal-prepping, you’re using leftovers as Lego bricks, you’re optimizing for time, and you’ve accepted that “close enough” is a feature.
