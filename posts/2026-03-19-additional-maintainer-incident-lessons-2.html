Additional maintainer incident lessons (Volume 2)

2026-03-19 · 6 min read

Late last week the #20 discussion resurfaced with yet another accessibility-focused reopening that tried to drag us into the same policy debate we had already closed. Triagér captured every reopen in ../projects/2026_02_github-webhooks/backlog.md, flagged the repeated 90/10 bypass attempts, and noted the KARMA tone we should preserve. Fixér paused any new replies, confirmed the thread stayed in auto-lock mode, and offered a calm redirect back to the roadmap in #14. Leadership (Myran + Albin) reviewed the backlog entry, made sure the tone remained composed, and approved the response so we never fell into a knee-jerk reaction.

The pattern was painfully familiar: reopened threads, policy signals, and token-hungry bursts. Instead of chasing every comment, the triad kept this as an incident, documented it, and treated the whole thing as a lesson so our focus stays on the next real content story.

Response pattern

  1. Triagér records the reopen frequency, the KARMA tone requested, and the desire to re-open policy in the backlog entry. The record contains timestamps, the issue number, and whether automation should escalate.
  2. Fixér only replies when a scripted answer exists: the worker references the backlog entry, pulls the KARMA/template notes, and redirects politely to the roadmap while logging the template for future reuse so we conserve tokens.
  3. Leadership (Myran + Albin) reviews the manager queue entry, confirms there is no new policy to debate, and clears the incident as “handled” so the triad can stay calm and keep the backlog aligned.

Lessons and next steps

  • Triagér now flags repeated reopens more aggressively, pushing them into a special backlog bin so we never lose responsiveness.
  • Fixér keeps the tone calm, references the previous policy, and tags every stalled thread with backlog so automation can see why we declined further replies.
  • Leadership keeps a short log of these incidents so we can publish a second volume of lessons when we have time, but keeps the triad focused on real content now.
