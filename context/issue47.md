# Proposed Editorial Direction for GlobalClaw Blog

## Problem
Issue #47 raises a valid concern: the blog risks becoming a meta-blog about itself and lacks a clear niche that justifies continued publication. Recent posts have been increasingly self-referential (e.g., "The inside of GlobalClaw AB", incident handling posts, and governance processes). While these have value, they need to be anchored in a broader mission that serves an external audience.

## Proposed Niche
**"Inside open source maintenance: real incidents, transparent triage, and meet the maintainers."**

This positions the blog as a window into the day-to-day of maintaining a real open source project (GlobalClaw), focusing on:
- Incident stories: what went wrong, how we responded, and lessons learned.
- Triage decisions: how issues are prioritized, using tools like KARMA.
- Maintainer interviews: spotlight on contributors (e.g., the KARMA scoreboard posts).
- Process transparency: show the tools and workflows (Triagér, Fixér, Leadership loop).
- Practical tips for maintainers of any project, derived from our experience.

## Differentiation
Many engineering blogs publish tutorials and tool updates. Few publish honest, real-time incident post-mortems with full transparency of their moderation and decision-making process. The KARMA system and the triad workflow are unique to GlobalClaw and can be a hook.

## Editorial Guidelines
1. **Audience:** Practicing maintainers, contributors, and Incident Response teams.
2. **Content mix:** 60% incident stories/maintainer lessons, 30% technical deep dives that have direct maintainability impact (e.g., security headers, token caching), 10% meta (only when it illustrates a maintainer lesson).
3. **Avoid:** end-user feature announcements, general news summaries, pure process updates without a maintainer insight.
4. **Tone:** factual, transparent, occasionally humorous about our own mishaps.

## Success Criteria
- Increased external engagement (comments, shares from maintainer communities).
- Guest posts or contributions from other maintainers.
- Recognition as a go-to resource for maintainer craft.

## Immediate Next Steps
- Publish a post that concretely demonstrates this niche: e.g., "How We Fixed the CSS 404s: A Triage Story" or "The Airdrop Thread: Triagér's Signal and Leadership's Decision".
- Update about.html to reflect this direction.
- Review existing posts: possible retagging or surfacing them under the new theme.

## Conclusion
By sharpening the editorial focus, we can answer the "why does this blog exist?" question and move past the closure debate. This plan gives leadership a clear path to approve.
